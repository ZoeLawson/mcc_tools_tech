Record Keeping & Research

Keeping a Health Binder

Here is a list of things that might be useful to keep in a health binder:

  • Insurance paperwork

  • Medical bills

  • Receipts for co-pays and prescriptions

  • Appointment summaries

  • Treatment / Modality Descriptions

  • Diagnosis Descriptions

  • Nutritional Guidelines for Special Diets

  • Business card holder for doctor/hospital/other business and medical cards

  • Copies of Medical Release and Health Proxy forms (Notarized).  Laws vary from state to state

  • A DNR (Do Not Resuscitate) if one is needed or desired.  Laws vary from state to state.

  • Physical therapy stretches & exercises

  • List of support contacts – support groups, emergency numbers, etc.

Tax information

Note:  I am not a tax professional!!  Please discuss any deductions with your accountant and/or refer to official IRS documentation.

Expenses to track include:

  • Mileage to and from doctor, pharmacy, lab

  • Medical equipment (crutches, glasses, hearing aids)

  • All deductibles and co-pays

  • All medications prescribed by a doctor

  • Nutritional Supplements:  You can include them ONLY if they are recommended by a medical practitioner as treatment for a specific medical condition diagnosed by a physician.  Otherwise, these items are taken to maintain your ordinary good health, and aren't for medical care.  Be sure to have a written record from your medical practitioner.

  • Difference in price for medically required food vs. conventional food (gluten-free, dairy-free, etc.)

    1. You can include the cost of special food in medical expenses only if:

      1. The food doesn't satisfy normal nutritional needs,

      2. The food alleviates or treats an illness, and

    2. The need for the food is substantiated by a physician.  The amount you can include in medical expenses is limited to the amount by which the cost of the special food exceeds the cost of a normal diet.

  • Medical conference admission fees relating to the chronic disease of the individual including lodging and transportation – but not meals (Revenue Ruling 2000-24)

Please reference the following IRS document for more information.  Publication 502:  Medical and Dental Expenses (including the Health Coverage Tax Credit)
